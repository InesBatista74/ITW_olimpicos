Welcome to the Summer Olympics of 2024

Here you can check all the athletes, modalities, countries, previous editions and future events!

The 2024 Summer Olympic Games (Les Jeux olympiques d'été de 2024), oficialmente chamados de Jogos da XXXIII Olimpíada (em francês: Jeux de la XXXIIIe Olympiade) and popularly known as Paris 2024, was an international sporting event held from July 26 to August 11, 2024 in France. The city of Paris was the main headquarters, although activities were carried out in 16 other French cities, in addition to having a sub-headquarters in Tahiti, in French Polynesia, the country's overseas territory.


The choice of Paris as the venue took place during the 131st Session of the International Olympic Committee (IOC), held in Lima, Peru, on September 13, 2017. After other candidatures withdrew, only Paris and Los Angeles remained, and the IOC decided to award the 2024 Games to Paris and the 2028 Games to Los Angeles. This will be the third time that Paris hosts the Summer Games, after 1900 and 1924, becoming the second city, after London (host in 1908, 1948 and 2012), to host the event three times. This event returns to the traditional four-year Olympic cycle, interrupted by the Olympic Games. 2020 in Tokyo, postponed due to the COVID-19 pandemic.


Among the new features, Paris 2024 will feature the debut of breakdance as an Olympic sport. It will also be the last event under the management of IOC President Thomas Bach. Despite the expectation of an equal number of male and female athletes women, the final list of entries revealed a slight male predominance. The total cost of the event was estimated at around 9 billion euros.
