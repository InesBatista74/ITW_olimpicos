History of the Modern Olympic Games

The Ancient Olympic Games originated in Ancient Greece, in Olympia, around 776 BC, and were held in honor of Zeus, the supreme god of Greek mythology. These games were one of the greatest religious and sporting events of the time, taking place every four years, a period known as an Olympiad. Only free Greek men were allowed to compete, participating in events such as races, wrestling, and the pentathlon. Besides showcasing strength and skill, athletes sought personal glory and honor for their city-states. The Games came to an end in the 4th century AD when they were banned by Roman Emperor Theodosius I as part of his efforts to abolish pagan practices and establish Christianity as the official religion of the Roman Empire.
